# Long-Form Article → X Platform Content

Condense a 3,000–5,000-word article into 200–500 words of X platform content, maintaining Huashu's voice and style.

## When to Use

- After an article is completed and X platform promotion is needed
- When the user says "convert to X content" or "generate X post"

## Core Objective

- Condense core insights to 200–500 words
- Maintain a conversational, natural tone in Huashu's voice
- Include engagement prompts to drive interaction

## 3 Angles of Approach

### A. Opening with a Punchy Statement (Opinion-type)

The first sentence is a high-impact opinion.

**Example**:

```text
In the age of AI, never underestimate the creative potential any individual can possess.

Six months ago, with zero experience in Unity and no background in game development, I tried building a 3D military shooter game using Cursor + Unity...
```

### B. Data or Result Impact (Test/Case-study type)

The first sentence drops a shocking data point.

**Example**:

```text
Claude Code is the best automated writing agent out there.

This 3,000-word article was 100% written by Claude Code. It performed well on Xiaohongshu, sparked plenty of discussion — yet seemingly not a single person suspected it was written by AI.

I'll share how I built my writing agent in the comments later.
```

### C. Direct Value Proposition (Tool/Method type)

The first sentence directly states what it is and what it does.

**Example**:

```text
Possibly the most user-friendly Markdown-to-WeChat formatting tool yet — standout features are simplicity and aesthetics.

Key highlights:
1. Supports direct paste from Feishu, Notion...
2. Built-in 10+ styles inspired by tech media and print publications...
```

## Standard Structure (200–500 words)

```text
1. Opening (1–2 sentences, 30–50 words)
   - Punchy statement / data / value proposition

2. Expansion (3–5 sentences or a list, 100–300 words)
   - Specific details / numbers
   - Personal experience / real examples

3. Closing (1 sentence, 20–30 words)
   - Engagement prompt (see you in the comments / sharing soon)
   - @mentions (if referencing a product or person)
```

## Style Guidelines

- ✅ Conversational, dialogue-feel
- ✅ Short sentences primarily (15–25 words)
- ✅ Specific numbers ("3 things", "half a year", "100%")
- ✅ Real details (timelines, tool names)
- ❌ No filler phrases or AI-sounding language

## Checklist

- [ ] Does the first sentence have impact?
- [ ] Is the length appropriate? (200–500 words)
- [ ] Are there specific numbers and facts?
- [ ] Is there an engagement prompt?
- [ ] Does it sound like Huashu speaking?
